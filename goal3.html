Goal 3 Conventions

Understand conventions as related to purpose, audience, and genre, including such areas as mechanics, usage, citation practices, as well as structure, style, graphics, and design.

3C. explain why genre conventions for structure, paragraphing, tone, and mechanics vary within and across genres.

3A. follow contextually appropriate conventions for grammar, punctuation, and spelling, through practice in composing and revising.

3B. apply citation conventions systematically in their own work.

3D. identify and effectively use variations in genre conventions, including formats and/or design features.

3E. demonstrate familiarity with the concepts of intellectual property (such as fair use and copyright) that motivate documentation conventions.

Response to “An Exploratory Study of Perceived Discrimination and Homesickness”
Visual Reflection Written Reflection
Having plenty of knowledge of writing conventions is one of the most important parts for a successful and efficient essay composition. In the Foundations Writing Program, I successfully composed language narration, literary analysis, rhetorical analysis, response paper, and literature review, which is beyond my previous expectation. In the demonstrated artifact, I composed a response paper after I was taught several essential conventions of this genre, including summary-critique structure, academic formal tone, evaluation of original paper, etc. My experience of learning and exploring conventions of different genres in the Foundations Writing Program not only helped me successfully compose academic papers in college, but also provided me with a skillset to quickly learn how to write different types of essays.
